## Lessons for Next Year

### The Big One: Fixture Awareness

The leader's rotation strategy was fundamentally **fixture-driven** — picking players from home teams or teams with easier matchups. This was the single biggest differentiator between our approaches.

**Examples where fixture awareness cost me:**
- **R5 Hooker**: I picked Turner (Scotland, away in Dublin, 4 pts) instead of Sheehan (Ireland, home, 43 pts). The leader brought Sheehan back specifically for the home fixture.
- **R3 Half-backs**: I picked White (Scotland, away in Cardiff, 12 pts) and Garbisi (Italy, away in Paris, 5 pts). The leader had Dupont (France, home vs Italy, 41 pts) and Russell (Scotland... but still managed 35). Home half-backs in dominant wins = big points.

**The leader's rule**: Keep the core spine, but rotate the 3-4 flex spots towards players from teams with home advantage or favourable matchups. Don't rotate based on the previous round's score — rotate based on **next week's fixture**.

### Specific Lessons

1. **Don't drop Dupont.** He's set-and-forget at scrum-half (155 pts across 5 rounds).
2. **Rotate fly-half based on expected winning margin — they live and die by kick points.** Conversions and penalties stack up fast in big wins. Pick the kicker from the team expected to win by the most. This is why Jalibert scored 87 at home vs Wales (R2) but only 14 away vs Ireland (R4) — same player, different fixture. Garbisi away in Paris (R3) was always going to be a disaster.

   | Fly-Half | Fixture | Result | Pts |
   |----------|---------|--------|-----|
   | Jalibert | France home vs Wales | Big win | **87** |
   | Ford | England home vs Wales | Big win | **65** |
   | Russell | Scotland home vs Wales | Win | **37** |
   | Garbisi | Italy home vs England | Win | **30** |
   | Jalibert | France away vs Ireland | Tough | **14** |
   | Garbisi | Italy away vs France | Heavy loss | **5** |

   **Rule**: Never pick the fly-half of a team travelling away to a stronger nation.

3. **Invest in hooker based on fixtures.** Not a throwaway slot — Kelleher/Sheehan can score 35-43 in a home game. Rotate premium hookers by home/away rather than sticking with a budget pick.
4. **Identify value props early.** Carre was the clear best prop from R3 data — pick him up and keep him.
5. **Limit changes to 3-4 per round.** The leader made small, targeted swaps. My R3 overhaul (6-7 changes) was too drastic and it backfired catastrophically.
6. **Stick with consistent performers.** McCloskey (27-40 every round) and Dupont (21-41) should never be dropped. The floor matters as much as the ceiling.
7. **Second row matters more than you think.** Chessum's R5 (53) and Guillard's R1 (42) show that locks can be difference-makers. Don't neglect this slot.
8. **Don't permanently bin players after one bad round.** Sheehan scored 4 in R2, so I dropped him for good. The leader dropped him too — but brought him back for R5's home fixture (43 pts). Rotate based on fixtures, not grudges.
